Key Destinations
Bangkok

The bustling capital, known for grand palaces, temples (Wat Pho, Wat Arun), floating markets, and vibrant nightlife.

Chiang Mai

A northern city famous for its old temples, mountainous landscapes, and annual festivals like Yi Peng (Lantern Festival).

Phuket

Thailand's largest island, renowned for its beaches, luxury resorts, and lively entertainment options.

Ayutthaya

A UNESCO World Heritage site, showcasing the ruins of an ancient capital filled with temples and statues.

Krabi

A coastal province offering scenic limestone cliffs, stunning beaches, and island hopping adventures (Phi Phi Islands).

Cultural Etiquette
Respect for Monarchie

Thais deeply respect their monarchy. Avoid any form of criticism.

Temple Etiquette

Always dress modestly when visiting temples. Shoulders and knees should be covered, and shoes must be removed before entering.

Greetings

The traditional Thai greeting is the "wai" (palms pressed together in a prayer-like gesture). Use this gesture to show respect.

Shoes Off Indoors

It’s customary to remove shoes before entering homes and some businesses.

Head and Feet

Avoid touching anyone’s head and pointing your feet at people or sacre

Local Cuisine
  • Pad Thai - Stir-fried noodles with shrimp or chicken, tofu, peanuts, and lime.
  • Tom Yom Goong - Spicy shrimp soup with lemongrass, lime leaves, and chili.
  • Som Tum - A tangy and spicy green papaya salad.
  • Massaman Curry - A rich, mild curry often made with chicken or beef, peanuts, and potatoes.
  • Sticky Rice with Mango - A popular Thai dessert made with sweet glutinous rice and fresh mango slices.
  • Street Food - Bangkok and Chiang Mai are especially famous for street food markets, offering everything from grilled meats to exotic fruits.
Practical Travel Information
Visas

Many nationalities can enter Thailand visa-free for short stays (up to 30 days). Check the Thai Embassy’s website for the latest visa rules.

Getting Around

Taxis and tuk-tuks are widely available in cities. Thailand also has an extensive bus network, trains, and domestic flights for intercity travel.

Health and Safety

It’s recommended to drink bottled water and be cautious with street food if you have a sensitive stomach. Carry sunscreen and bug repellent.

Currency Exchange

Currency can be easily exchanged at banks, airports, or ATMs. Credit cards are accepted in most urban areas, but cash is still preferred in rural areas and for small purchases.

Internet and SIM Cards

Free Wi-Fi is common in hotels and cafés. SIM cards with data plans are inexpensive and available at airports or convenience stores.
